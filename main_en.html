Peer-to-Peer (P2P) and P2P Applications.

  1. What is Peer-to-Peer (P2P)?

    • P2P is a distributed network architecture where end systems (peers) communicate directly with each other without relying on a central server.

    • Each peer acts as both:

      • A client: Requesting services or resources.

      • A server: Providing services or resources.

    • Key Features:

      • No always-on central server: Peers connect to each other dynamically.

      • Decentralized: Control and resources are distributed among peers.

      • Scalability: When more peers join, they contribute to the overall resource pool, which increase service capacity.

      What is P2P

  2. How P2P Works:

    1. A peer joins the network and connects to other peers (neighbors).
    2. It can share files, processing power, or any other resources with other peers in the network.
    3. Resource sharing happen directly between peers, by passing centralized infrastructure.

  3. Examples of P2P Applications:

    1. File Sharing:

      • Examples : BitTorrent, LimeWire.
      • Peers share parts of a file, allowing efficient distribution. A peer downloads chunks of the file from multiple other peers and uploads chunks to others in return .

        • to get more knowledge about File Sharing Click Here!

    2. Content Distribution:

      • Examples : IPTV, decentralized video streaming platforms.
      • Distributes video and multimedia content by leveraging the resources of participating peers

    3. VoIP (Voice over IP):

      • Examples: Skype (early versions used a P2P architecture).
      • Peers exchange voice and video packets directly.

    4. Blockchain and Cryptocurrencies:

      • Examples: Bitcoin, Ethereum.
      • Uses P2P for decentralized transaction validation and record-keeping without a central authority.

    5. Gaming:

      • Examples: Multiplayer games using P2P for communication between players.
      • Instead of routing through a central server, peers connect directly, reducing latency.

    6. Distributed Computing:

      • Examples: SETI@Home, Folding@Home.
      • Peers contribute their computing resources to solve complex problems collaboratively.

  4. Advantages of P2P:

    • Scalability:Resources grow as new peers join the network.
    • Fault Tolerance: No single point of failure.
    • Cost Efficiency: No need for centralized servers.

  5. Challenges of P2P:

    • Security: Difficult to control malicious peers.
    • Data Integrity: Ensuring that data shared among peers is trustworthy.
    • Dynamic Topology:Peers may join or leave unpredictably, complicating resource management.
